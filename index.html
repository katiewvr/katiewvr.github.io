About me

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in the political science department at Tulane University. My research explores the domestic determinants of donor countries' foreign aid budgets, including the role of the welfare state, citizen-level cosmopolitanism, and the opinion-policy nexus.

I received a B.A. in Political Science and English from Tulane University, an M.A. in Social and Cultural Analysis from NYU, and an M.A. in Political Science en route from Tulane. Prior to beginning the Ph.D., I worked in publishing at Oxford University Press and in research communications at The Murphy Institute.
